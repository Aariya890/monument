Famous Monuments

Big Ben

Big Ben’s mighty clock tower is one of the most recognizable landmarks around the globe and one of England’s most iconic symbols. Perched on. the banks of the Thames alongside the Palace of Westminster, it’s impossible to miss while in London. The clock and tower are currently being refurbished but are set to unveil sometime in 2022.

Easter Island Moai

Large stone statues on Easter Island, built by the island's inhabitants over 1,000 years ago, thought to represent ancestors or deities. It is situated in Easter Island Statues, Chile.

Great Spinx of Giza

The Great Sphinx of Giza is a limestone statue of a reclining sphinx, a mythical creature with the head of a human and the body of a lion. It is situated on the Giza Plateau on the west bank of the Nile in Giza, Egypt.

Pyramid

Of all the ancient seven wonders of the world, the Great Pyramid of Giza is the only wonder that has survived to stand tall through thick and thin. Constructed between 2550 and 2490 B.C.

Leading Tower of Pisa

The Leaning Tower of Pisa, or simply the Tower of Pisa, is the campanile, or freestanding bell tower, of Pisa Cathedral.
